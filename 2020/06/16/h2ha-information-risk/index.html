How to Hack Information Risk

Published Tue, Jun 16, 2020 by Tony Towry

Photo by Photo by Thilak Lees on Unsplash

This article contains some of my advice on how to go deep and what can be done to start getting up to speed on Information Risk in your first 3 hours.

Mark the books you should read [10 minutes to order some books or add them to a queue]:

  • Measuring and Managing Information Risk: A FAIR Approach
  • How to Measure Anything in Cybersecurity Risk
  • Against the Gods: The Remarkable Story of Risk
  • Then just go ahead and dig into The Society of Information Risk Analysts Recommended Reading

I recommend picking these two up at the same time and reading them in the order listed. I think this is probably the fastest way to get up to speed on what’s happening in Information Risk.

Blogs you should read and update your RSS reader with [2 minutes to update your feed reader]:

  • Exploring Possibility Space
  • Tony Martin-Vague
  • The Risk Doctor

People you should follow [2 minutes to hit the follow button]:

  • @tdmv
  • @MrMeritology
  • @oracuk
  • @CxOSidekick
  • @jayjacobs
  • @osamasalah

If you can get on the sira-public mailing list, definitely do that [This might take some minutes…lets say 15]. I’ve learned a ton from lurking on that list. Many of the things I’ve learned are emerging ideas and honestly not likely to show up anywhere else.

Know something about what’s available in assessment and analysis methodologies COBIT, ISO, NIST, OCTAVE, FAIR, etc. Don’t worry about “cycle time” for a methodology without taking a look and attempting to work through one. What is the time-frame for utility? [120 minutes]

Familiarize yourself with the Cox Risk Matrix Theorem [30 minutes]. There’s quite a lot to see on both sides and it’s valuable to understand the terrain.
