Let's Talk About Extreme Value Theory

Getting a Sense of The Animal By Looking Closely At The Tail

Published Sun, Jun 2, 2019 by Tony Towry

Let’s talk about Extreme value theory - Wikipedia and information risk. I won’t throw down a bunch of R, because it’d basically just be a re-hash of the first reference listed below.

Instead, I’ll share my current thinking on where EVT might fit.

It fits when you’re interested in examining the end of the tail. When you might have a handful of extreme, low probability observations and you want to fit a distribution to those observations to help estimate a true maximum or for some reason think about just how extreme something might get.

This might be appropriate for discussions regarding insurable risk scenarios.

I additionally found interesting the risk concepts defined by Actuarial Credit Risk Accounting for expected loss, unexpected loss, and stress loss.

References I found useful:

  • Modelling large insurance claims using Extreme Value Theory in R - Laurence Holmes (I believe the author meant to set a threshold at 10,000 not 1,000…)
  • FRM: Extreme Value Theory (EVT) - Intro - YouTube from Bionic Turtle
