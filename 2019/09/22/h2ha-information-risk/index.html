How to Hack Information Risk

Published Sun, Sep 22, 2019 by Tony Towry

This article contains some of my advice on how to go deep and get up to speed on Information Risk.

#Books you should read:# * Measuring and Managing Information Risk: A FAIR Approach * How to Measure Anything in Cybersecurity Risk * Against the Gods: The Remarkable Story of Risk

I recommend picking these two up at the same time and reading them in the order listed. I think this is probably the fastest way to get up to speed.

#Blogs you should read and update your RSS reader with:# * Exploring Possibility Space * Tony Martin-Vague * The Risk Doctor

#People you should follow:# * @tdmv * @MrMeritology * @oracuk * @CxOSidekick * @jayjacobs * @osamasalah

If you can get on the sira-public mailing list, definitely do that. I’ve learned a ton from lurking on that list. Many of the things I’ve learned are emerging ideas and honestly not likely to show up anywhere else.

Know something about what’s available in assessment and analysis methodologies COBIT, ISO, NIST, OCTAVE, FAIR, etc. Don’t worry about “cycle time” for a methodology without taking a look and attempting to work through one. What is the time-frame for utility?

Familiarize yourself with the Cox Risk Matrix Theorem. There’s quite a lot to see on both sides and it’s valuable to understand the terrain.
