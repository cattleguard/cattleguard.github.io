If You’re Going to Fail To Scale, Don’t - Part II

A warning against infosec gates.

Published Sat, Jul 15, 2017 by Tony Towry

First, if you haven’t read Part I here’s the link. Now, on to Part II.

If You’re Going to Fail To Scale, Don’t - Part II

People hate to wait.

In Kaiser Fung’s book Numbers Rule Your World he discusses two congestion problem case studies. One relates to the grueling wait times at Disney attractions. The other is a study on ramp metering in Minneapolis.

Now, if you’re not familiar with ramp metering here’s the gist. A stoplight is placed at the end of an on ramp which regulates how many cars are allowed onto a highway at a given time. The idea being that the number of required slowdowns and wrecks decreases as cars have appropriate distance.

I experienced this in LA and found it consistent with the study, in that the result was a perceived more pleasant drive. Far more pleasant I’d say than the morning commute here in Oklahoma City.

Waiting sucks. Unreliable waits suck more.

If you only have capacity to deliver for the little green dots, maybe it’s time to consider letting the orange and red guys continue about their business.

What you DO NOT want to do is deadline all of the dots to rush the gate at the same time and watch them pile up in a fiery mess.

There is a cost to queuing and it’s not necessarily a soft cost. Meter the assets you want to address in a way that avoids collisions and allows those that would be endlessly waiting to continue on with business.

This may require treating an asset group as an internal statement of work that is approved on the interval.

Don’t be afraid to model reality, alter perception, under promise and over deliver. Oh, and go get Kaiser’s book, it’s a pretty fun read.
